Agentic AI
Why now
AI is moving from chat to agents that actually do the work. The models are ready, and most industries haven't been touched yet.
In Canada
Cohere builds world-class AI models in Toronto and is valued at US$7B. Vector, Mila and Amii lead the research. Behind them, Ottawa is building $2B of AI compute, including a public supercomputer.
Build
Pick one painful workflow in law, health, finance or government. Build the agent that does it start to finish, and the tests that prove it works.

Quantum
Why now
Quantum is leaving the lab. The early winners in hardware, software and security are being decided now.
In Canada
Canada has built four billion-dollar quantum companies: D-Wave, Xanadu, Photonic and Nord Quantique. Only the US can match that. Add a $360M national strategy and Waterloo's Quantum Valley.
Build
Tools that move today's encryption to quantum-safe standards, a circuit visualizer, or experiments on PennyLane, Xanadu's open source framework.

Robotics
Why now
AI is getting a body. Robots for warehouses, farms and job sites are finally getting good, and the startups behind them raised a record US$14B in 2025.
In Canada
Sanctuary AI builds humanoid robots in Vancouver. Waabi in Toronto just raised US$750M, one of the largest rounds in Canadian history, to put 25,000 self-driving vehicles on Uber.
Build
You don't need to build a robot. Build the software: simulation tools, remote operation interfaces, or the vision systems robots depend on.

AI for Science
Why now
AlphaFold turned years of protein research into minutes and won the 2024 Nobel Prize in Chemistry. Every field of science is about to get faster.
In Canada
Mila runs a dedicated AI for science program. Valence Labs does AI drug discovery from inside Mila, and 16 of the top 20 pharma companies use BenchSci's tools.
Build
A tool that designs proteins, an assistant that makes sense of lab data, or an agent that reads papers and suggests the next experiment.
